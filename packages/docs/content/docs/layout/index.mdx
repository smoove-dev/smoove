---
title: Layout
description: Why layout matters in dynamic video, and how flex reflow keeps a frame aligned as the content changes.
---

A static design is positioned once. A dynamic video is positioned on every
frame. The label you pin at `x: 200` fits at frame 0 and overflows at frame 90
when the word count doubles. The three cards you spaced by hand become five when
the data changes. A number counting from 9 up to 1000 grows three digits wider
as it runs. Hand-placed coordinates assume content that holds still, and in
motion graphics it rarely does.

That is the problem layout solves. Instead of writing `x`/`y` for every node,
you describe the structure once (a row, a column, gaps, padding) and let the
engine measure and place the children. When a size, a count, or a gap changes,
the layout recomputes and everything stays aligned.

## Reflow every tick

`Sequence._apply` runs the layout on every top-level `Flex` or `Block` child
each frame. Layout is not a one-time setup step; it is part of the animation
loop. Drive a gap, a padding, or a child's size from the frame and the siblings
move themselves to make room.

```ts
seq.register((frame) => {
  row.setAttrs({ gap: interpolate(frame, [0, 60], [8, 48]) });
  // every child re-spaces itself; no per-child x math
});
```

This is the part raw `Konva.*` nodes can't express. A `Konva.Group` has no notion
of "share the leftover space" or "track the container at 50%". The core wrappers
add flexbox on top of every shape, so the structure survives whatever the content
does at frame N.

## The two building blocks

- [Flex](/docs/layout/flex): a group that arranges its children in rows and
  columns with flexbox props, and reflows every tick.
- [Block](/docs/layout/block): a `Flex` that also paints a background, border,
  corner radius, and shadow. The box you reach for when a group of children needs
  to look like a card or a panel.

Reach for layout whenever you mean structure ("these sit in a row, evenly
spaced") rather than a fixed point on the canvas. Pin coordinates only for the
things that genuinely live at one spot.
